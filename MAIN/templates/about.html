The Office

The creation of MSU Intellectual Property Office (MIPO) is one of the rights and responsibilities of all Research and Development Institutes/Institutions (RDIs) to establish and strengthen their IP Management and Technology Licensing Offices given by the rules and regulations implementing Republic act 10055 (IRR of RA 10055, Rules 10, 24 and 25) issued jointly by DOST and IPOPHIL as the national government agencies mandated to promulgate such IRR. It is also MSU Marawi OVCRED's response to CHED directive for SUCs and HEIs to establish their own IP Policy and the systemwide approval of MSU-lIT IP Policy and from which the BOR approved creation of MSU-IIT IPU was based. The MIPO will function to protect and manage all IPs and IPRs of the university main campus and make them available to the public and private sector for the benefit of the community and the country.

Mission

MSU Intellectual Property Office (MIPO) is committed to promote intellectual property awareness, safeguard all IPs and IPRS by filing for protection and facilitate IP activities which include generation, preservation, and transmission of knowledge, utilization and commercialization, and techno-transfer of IPs.

Vision

A leading technological hub in BARMM dedicated to serving the University and constituents and the local IP developers for the protection of all Intellectual Property rights and foster technology transfer of IPs to public and private sectors.

Objectives

● Promote and facilitate the IP program development of the institution;

● Integrate IP programs and policies in all scholarly endeavors of the institution;

● Conduct IP mapping and assessment in MSU and its service areas;

● Identify possible IPs for possible technology transfer and commercialization; and

● Manage IP in MSU and its service area

Goals

MIPO aims to establish dynamic involvement of all University constituents and the public in various IP programs initiated by the Office.
